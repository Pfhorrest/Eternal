Cronus-class Fusion Pistols

The successor to the wildly popular and long-lived Zeus-class Fusion Pistol, the Cronus-class is the new standard sidearm of the UESC military. Featuring much improved plasma flow control, the Cronus-class packs all the power of its predecessor into a sleek, stable weapon that can safely be wielded single-handedly. This, combined with an automated reloading mechanism capable of interfacing directly with an officer's portable pattern buffer systems, can enable skilled officers to dual-wield the Cronus-class, providing highly effective plasma fire coverage in intense combat situations.
Ouranus-class Fusion Cannon

Designed specifically as an anti-armor weapon in the war with the Pfhor, the Ouranus-class Fusion Cannon is a high powered iteration of Cronus-class technology. With a much higher rate of fire and an elongated barrel for improved control, the Ouranus-class can provide more effective plasma fire coverage than even two Cronus-class Fusion Pistols. It also retains the secondary overload-fire feature from the older Zeus-class model, enabling an officer to charge a more powerful shot before discharging the weapon. As with the Zeus-class, this feature is unstable and prone to violent discharge if misused.
Aggregate-issue Shock Staff

This is the standard issue weapon typically wielded by the lower ranking, more lightly armored members of the Pfhor Aggregate caste, commonly known as "Fighters". It is not only a melee weapon akin to a taser wand, capable of delivering debilitating electrical shocks on impact, but features a mechanism for discharging coherent balls of electricity via an unknown plasma-ionization mechanism. Like most Pfhor weapons it is usable only when enabled by a Pfhor IFF code; thus all contemporary UESC personnel carry replica transponders to facilitate the appropriation of enemy weapons in the field.
Aggregate-issue Havok Rifle

This is the standard issue weapon typically wielded by the higher ranking, more heavily armored members of the Pfhor Aggregate caste, commonly known as "Troopers". It bears much resemblance to late-model assault rifle / grenade launcher hybrids, including vacuum-fire capabilities. Rapidly firing eighty-four rounds per magazine and seven grenades per clip, the Havok Rifle lives up to its name. Like most Pfhor weapons it is usable only when enabled by a Pfhor IFF code; thus all contemporary UESC personnel carry replica transponders to facilitate the appropriation of enemy weapons in the field.
Willful-issue Napalm Cannon

One of the two weapons regularly issued to members of the Pfhor Willful caste, commonly known as "Enforcers", the "N-Cannon" (as it is called) is used by the Pfhor primarily for coersive control of their slaves or "Conditioned Ranks" on planetside field missions, where it's high accuracy and rate of fire, high painfulness, but low damage per shot enable the Pfhor Enforcers to effectively reprimand their slaves from afar. Unlike Aggregate-issue weapons, the N-Cannon and other Willful-issue weapons are fully functional without any activation code.
Willful-issue Scatter Rifle

One of the two weapons regularly issued to members of the Pfhor Willful caste, commonly known as "Enforcers", the "S-Rifle" (as it is called) is used by the Pfhor primarily for coersive control of their slaves or "Conditioned Ranks" on indoor or shipborne missions. Its broad spray pattern and high rate of fire provide for excellent crowd control, and a shotgun-like secondary function also provides excellent protection to the user should said crowds prove exceptionally unruly. Unlike Aggregate-issue weapons, the S-Rifle and other Willful-issue weapons are fully functional without any activation code.
ZX-class Wave Motion Cannon

A rare weapon of Jjaro origin, most of which were destroyed by the Pfhor in their purges of Jjaro technology, the ZX-class Wave Motion Cannon operates via currently unknown methods to project an intense gravitic shear forward from the user, which causes severe localized tidal stress on everything in its path, effectively destroying any fragile organic or mechanical systems in a single pass. This weapon is believed to be powered by a zero-point energy generator which converts matter, via Hawking radiation, into pure energy to power the gravitic field generators.
ZX-class Gravitronic Blades

A rare weapon of Jjaro origin, most of which were destroyed by the Pfhor in their purges of Jjaro technology, the ZX-class Gravitronic Blades operate via currently unknown methods to sustain an intense, localized gravitic shear, exposure to which causes severe tidal stress, effectively destroying any fragile organic or mechanical systems in a single pass. This weapon is believed to be powered by a zero-point energy generator which converts matter, via Hawking radiation, into pure energy to power the gravitic field generators.
